## Stroke is not line weight in print

On screen, a 0.4 mm stroke looks elegant. On wood at high speed, it can **disappear** or char wider than drawn.

## Three failure modes

### Too thin (< 0.15 mm on wood)

Beam overlap vaporizes the line; corners round off; paint fill bleeds across the gap.

### Too thick (> 0.8 mm on small coasters)

Double-line borders merge visually; customers read it as one fat ring, not a frame.

### Ignoring material

Acrylic tolerates finer strokes than open-grain walnut. Cast acrylic needs flame-polish awareness; wood needs masking discipline.

## Practical starting points (CO₂)

| Material | Border stroke |
|----------|---------------|
| 3 mm birch plywood | 0.25–0.40 mm |
| Cast acrylic | 0.20–0.35 mm |
| MDF paint-fill plaques | 0.35–0.50 mm |

Tune in the [Frame Builder](/tool) and test on scrap.

## After cut: paint fill

Thicker strokes give paint a wider channel — good for contrast, bad for fine medallion rings. Match stroke to your fill needle or brush workflow.
