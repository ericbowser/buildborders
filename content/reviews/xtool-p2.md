## Why we care about the P2 for borders

Execute & Engrave runs bordered plaques daily. The P2 matters because **alignment and enclosure** affect margin on every job — not just raw wattage.

## Border workflow fit

- **Camera preview** lines up pre-cut blanks with SVG frames imported from our [Frame Builder](/tool)
- **55W CO₂** cuts 3–6 mm acrylic frame rings in one pass at production speeds
- **Pass-through** helps longer sign borders that exceed bed depth

## Where it beats open-frame CO₂

| Task | P2 advantage |
|------|----------------|
| Repeat oval plaques | Camera + fixed enclosure reduce setup drift |
| Shared workshop | Beam enclosure is a safety win |
| Mixed materials same day | Less fuss than tuning an open chassis in a busy shop |

## Where OMTech still wins

If you already own LightBurn workflows, exhaust, and jigs — [OMTech 55W](/review/omtech-55w) delivers similar cut quality at lower MSRP. See [direct comparison](/compare/xtool-p2/omtech-55w).

## LightBurn integration

Export grouped SVG from `/tool`, import to LightBurn, map `cut` layer to line mode. Full walkthrough: [SVG for LightBurn](/guides/svg-for-lightburn).

## Bottom line

Choose P2 when **production throughput and alignment** justify the premium. Choose OMTech or S1 when budget or learning curve matters more.
